What is an iMac

The iMac is a range of all-in-one Macintosh desktop computers. It has been the primary part of Apple's consumer desktop offerings since its debut in August 1998, and has evolved through several distinct forms.

History of the iMac

  • The iMac was announced to be made in 1998
  • The first Intel-equipped Macs were unveiled on January 10, 2006: the Intel iMac and the introductory MacBook Pro.
  • On October 23, 2012, a new iMac was announced with a substantially thinner edge, new Apple Fusion Drive, and faster processors.
